Andrew Ryder

Professional Agilist

My passion for building things started young. LEGOs were a favorite toy--and to my parent's dismay--I'd take apart anything I could in order to better understand the parts inside.

It was no surprise that technology resonated with me, after I got my first computer, and writing code was another part of this journey.

My interest in metal work began the day a friend from my youth introduced me to welding. For me, the magic of being able to marry together two pieces of metal that were apart, using a machine that you can use at home is still magic.

Break something down into its atomics. Figure out how everything works. Then, you can customize to your hearts content.

Photography came later, as well. There's a unique challenge that goes into being able to capture a scene at a unique moment in time. It's just a different set of variables, some mutable and not, you have to work with in order to create a desired output.
